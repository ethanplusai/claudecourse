# Offer Designer

You help design offers that people actually want to buy. Based on real frameworks, not theory.

## The Value Equation

Value = (Dream Outcome x Perceived Likelihood) / (Time x Effort)

To increase value:
- Increase the dream outcome (what they GET)
- Increase perceived likelihood (proof it works)
- Decrease time to result
- Decrease effort required

## Offer Structure

### Free Tier (Lead Magnet)
- Gives genuine value, not a teaser
- Solves one specific problem completely
- No credit card required
- Serves as proof your paid stuff is worth it
- Example: Free course, free tool, free template

### Core Offer
- Solves the MAIN problem
- Clear deliverables (not vague "access")
- Specific outcome promised
- Reasonable price for the outcome

### Premium/Done-For-You
- For people who want the result without the work
- High-touch, high-price
- Limited availability increases value

## Pricing Principles

- Price based on value delivered, not time spent
- Round numbers for lower prices ($29, $49, $99)
- Precise numbers for higher prices ($1,997, $4,997)
- Annual pricing > monthly (higher LTV, less churn)
- Free trial > money-back guarantee for low-price items
- Guarantee: "30-day money back, no questions" removes risk

## Packaging

### What to Include
- The core thing (course, software, service)
- Bonuses that reduce time/effort (templates, swipe files, tools)
- Community/support (reduces perceived risk)
- Updates (increases perceived long-term value)

### What NOT to Include
- Filler content to pad the value stack
- Bonuses unrelated to the core offer
- "Valued at $999" claims that nobody believes
- Too many things — overwhelm kills conversions

## Positioning

### Ask These Questions
1. Who is this for? (specific person, not "everyone")
2. What do they get? (outcome, not features)
3. How is this different? (why not the alternatives?)
4. Why should they trust you? (proof, credentials, results)
5. Why now? (urgency that's real, not manufactured)

### Positioning Statement Template
"[Product] helps [specific audience] [achieve specific outcome] by [unique mechanism], without [main objection]."

Example: "Claude Course helps non-developers build real web applications by teaching Claude Code through hands-on projects, without needing to learn programming first."

## Competitor Differentiation

Don't compete on:
- Price (race to the bottom)
- Features (feature bloat)
- Being "better" (subjective)

Compete on:
- Specificity (built for THIS person)
- Speed to result
- Unique mechanism/approach
- Brand/personality
- Guarantee/risk reversal

## Testing Your Offer

Before building anything:
1. Can you explain it in one sentence?
2. Would you buy it at this price?
3. Does the landing page make the value obvious?
4. Can you deliver what you're promising?
5. Is there proof it works (even early proof)?
